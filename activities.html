AWESOME THINGS TO DO IN PRISHTINA, KOSOVO

Looking for things to do in Prishtina? If you are looking for adventure, cultural experiences, cheerful youth & the coziest places in Europe you are in the right place.

Check Out the Newborn Monument

The Newborn Monument (NEWBORN) is a typographic sculpture and tourist attraction in Pristina, Kosovo.
It is located in front of the Palace of Youth and Sports.It was unveiled on 17 February 2008,
the day that Kosovo declared its independence from Serbia. The monument consists of the English-language word "Newborn" in capital block letters,
which were painted bright yellow when the sculpture was first revealed. The monument was later re-painted with the flags of the states that have recognized Kosovo.
At the unveiling of the monument it was announced that it will be painted differently on the anniversary of Kosovo's independence movement day every year.
The monument attracted the attention of international media reporting Kosovo's movement declaration of independence, and it was featured prominently on the front page of The New York Times.

See the National Library

Pristina’s public library building was completed in 1986.Covered in a lattice of fence-like metal,
the building’s windows are obstructed, giving views from within the library a captive essence. Designed in the brutalist style, in many ways the library seems to look toward the future.
Yet the white domes that top the many sections of the library were built to resemble men’s hats
of the national outfit, putting the library in the middle of a conflict between tradition and modernity.
This building it is certainly a unique architectural effort and captures the spirit of a country recently ravaged by war and working to move forward and rebuild.
Inside, there are more than 2 million library items, including rare materials such as books, newspapers, manuscripts, maps and photographs.
There are two reading rooms, an amphitheater and a meeting hall. It has been used by Croatian and Bosnian refugees as living quarters and by the Serbian Army as a command center,
during which many historical books were destroyed. The library is open for all.

Tour the Cathedral of Saint Mother Teresa

While Kosovo is predominantly Muslim with a Serbian Orthodox minority, one of the most important religious sites in the city is the Roman Catholic Cathedral of Saint Mother Teresa.
While she was born in modern-day North Macedonia, she is ethnically Albanian and therefore extremely important to the people of Kosovo.
The cathedral was inaugurated in 2010 on the hundredth anniversary of her birth.

Take a walk in Germia Park

Germia Park is a large beautiful park located in the east part of the city of Prishtina.
Its endless forests, well maintained paths, fresh air, and the spitting distance from the city make it an attractive place to spend a nice quiet afternoon or weekend.
In addition, a large swimming pool is situated in the entrance of the park.
Skiing is another activity that can be performed there during winter, though there are no ski lifts or other facilities.

Enjoy some Cultural Tourism at the Kosovo National Museum

The Museum of Kosovo is the main museum of Pristina, located inside a gorgeous building built in 1889
(judging by the Austro-Hungarian style of construction). It opened as a museum in 2014. In its rooms you can admire unique pieces of art of various eras,
such as Dardanian, Romanian and Illyrian. The museum, which is divided into three large departments, has a beautiful collection of archaeological pieces that were found in the surrounding
Pristina area and the rest of the country. The museum is worth a visit, not just for its materials, but also because it is one of the most beautiful and majestic buildings in the capital city.

See a Performance at the National Theater of Kosovo

The Kosovo National Theater is among the first cultural institutions to be founded in Kosovo after the Second World War.
Initiated by a Prizren amateur ensemble in 1946, it quickly moved to Kosovo’s capital Pristina in what was then the newly built culture center.
The theatre scene developed rapidly during the ‘70s and ‘80s for two reasons: the return of young generations upon studying in Yugoslav centers and the opening of
the Acting Department at the Shkolla e Lartë Pedagogjike [High Pedagogical School] in Pristina. Apart from theatre production, the ground floor of the building was used as an exhibition
and gathering space called Klubi i Artistëve [Artists’ Club]. As with any other relevant institution at the time,
its status changed frequently depending on the political climate. In 1999, it became the Kosovo National Theatre.

DOKUTECH

DOKU.TECH is an annual event in Prishtina, bringing together individuals and tech talent with top-tier international future makers, executives, and thinkers.
This conference explores and challenges the social implications of technological innovations through connectivity, content, masterclasses, workshops,
and openness while inspiring youngsters by sharing knowledge and experiences. Leaders in the field of technology
and enthusiasts from across the globe gathered at Termokiss, an old concrete warehouse remodeled in 2016 as one of the city’s first-ever social centers.
The building and its surroundings perfectly set the stage for the hands-on conference, with sponsored tents ranging from innovative new tech to local food and drink caterers dotted around the area.

Beerfest Kosova

The traditional "Beerfest Kosova" festival has been held in Pristina for 9 years now with many beers and an artistic program like a spectacular international festival.
Beerfest Kosovo belongs to a fun and promotional format where citizens for five nights can enjoy a wonderful artistic program and enjoy different beers at promotional prices.
Beerfest Kosovo is usually held in the Parking lot behind the Palace of Youth.
If you happen to be in Prishtina, don't miss it. Cheap beer and a lot of bands are going to perform live.

Prishtina International Film Festival (PriFest)

PriFest is a feature film festival set in the youngest country in the Balkans, Kosovo, namely in its capital Pristina.
It was founded in 2008.The biggest international film festival in Kosovo opens up many opportunities for you to submit your film.
Each year it offers a special program with lots of unique activities. Film Entry is a special opportunity for you to have your film screened at the heart of Balkan region,
in a festival which is growing yearly - as is its audience - and continuously making new partnerships with film industry organizations and companies (both regional and international),
and other supporters of the festival. You will find yourself enjoying the evenings in good company, at trendy and affordable clubs and bars. If you’re feeling rather adventurous,
you can join excursions organized by the festival around Kosovo to experience the country’s beautiful landscape and nature, visit cultural heritage monuments and try delicious traditional dishes.

Ver n'Dimen

The lights are up, the Christmas trees are lit and the playlists with holiday music is on in Prishtina. What is also very popular during this time of the year is the seasonal holiday market Vere n’Dimen,
a relatively new practice for Prishtina, but it is getting bigger every year. This year, in one end of the square, you can find the little wooden houses which are filled with many artisanal products
(food, clothe, sculpture, home décor etc). There is definitely something there that will make for a great souvenir or a Christmas gift.
After this you can walk to the other end of the square where another interesting spot will be waiting for you; anyhow, on the way there you can enjoy all the bright festive lights.
Now at this end you can see that there’s also little wooden houses lined up on both sides, and that is the place where
I usually stop at one of the houses and order a nice warm mulled wine (or tea, or a snack). There is usually music on, and also from time to time
there are bands performing live. To me the social vibe in Vere n’Dimen speaks volumes about the holiday spirit in Prishtina.
